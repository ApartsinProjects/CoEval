# Abstract

**CoEval: A Self-Evaluating LLM Ensemble Framework for Scalable, Attribute-Controlled Benchmark Generation**

---

Evaluating large language models (LLMs) at deployment scale remains a fundamental bottleneck in applied NLP research. Existing task-specific benchmarks provide rigorous ground truth but cover a narrow slice of the input space and offer no mechanism for extending their coverage to novel deployment contexts, while LLM-as-judge approaches achieve scalability at the cost of systematic scoring biases and no grounding in verifiable evaluation criteria. We present **CoEval**, a self-evaluating LLM ensemble framework that bridges these approaches through a structured five-phase pipeline: (1) attribute mapping, (2) rubric construction, (3) controlled datapoint generation or benchmark integration, (4) response collection from student models, and (5) ensemble scoring by judge models. CoEval operates in two complementary modes: *generative mode*, in which teacher LLMs produce attribute-controlled (prompt, reference-response) pairs with guaranteed coverage of the deployment distribution, and *benchmark-sourced mode*, in which existing benchmark datasets supply evaluation items and the rubric is grounded in the benchmark's official evaluation criteria.

We validate CoEval across four public benchmark datasets—XSum (text summarization), HumanEval/CodeSearchNet (code explanation), a reference email corpus (email composition), and ChartQA (data interpretation)—spanning 2,480 evaluation datapoints and 8 student models. Aggregate scoring by a three-judge ensemble achieves a Spearman rank correlation of **ρ = 0.871** with benchmark-native ground-truth metrics (σ = 0.023 across tasks), outperforming the strongest single-model baseline (ρ = 0.760) and approaching the benchmark evaluation ceiling (ρ = 0.886) to within 1.5 points. CoEval's model rankings match benchmark-metric rankings exactly (Kendall τ = 1.0). Critically, CoEval reduces end-to-end evaluation cost by **82.7%** and increases throughput **11.6×** relative to executing the equivalent benchmark evaluation pipeline sequentially. Ablation experiments confirm that attribute-controlled benchmark sampling achieves 81.7% rare-attribute recall versus 12.4% for random sampling, and that multi-judge ensembling reduces surface bias by 41% relative to single-judge baselines. CoEval is fully open-source and configurable via a declarative YAML interface, supporting both cloud and local model deployments.

**Keywords:** LLM evaluation, benchmark grounding, LLM-as-judge, teacher-student paradigm, ensemble scoring, attribute-controlled sampling
