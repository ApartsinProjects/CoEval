1. Teacher Effectiveness

Higher scores indicate more discriminative questions that reveal student ability differences. Effective teachers are highlighted.

About this section
Table: Each row is a teacher model ranked by the selected formula. Teachers above the effectiveness threshold are highlighted green and used in student ranking.
V1 (Variance) = Var(per-student avg scores) × coverage. High V1 means the teacher’s items produce very different scores across students — a strong discriminative signal.
S2 (Spread) = mean(|score − mean|) × coverage. Mean absolute deviation: robust alternative to variance, less affected by outliers.
R3 (Range) = (max − min) × coverage. Simplest spread measure: at least one student scored high and one scored low.
Chart: Bar chart of the selected formula value per teacher. Green bars = effective (above threshold); blue = inactive.

2. Judge Agreement

Higher agreement = judge is more aligned with consensus scoring. Consensus judges pass the current threshold.

About this section
Table: Each row is a judge model with inter-judge agreement scores. SPA = Simple Percent Agreement (fraction of pairs with identical scores); WPA = Weighted Percent Agreement (weighted by unit count); κ = Cohen's Kappa (chance-corrected agreement). Judges above the current agreement threshold are highlighted as “consensus judges” and are used in the student ranking computation.
Chart: Bar chart comparing agreement values per judge for the selected metric (SPA / WPA / Kappa).
Kappa guide: <0 worse than chance; 0–0.2 slight; 0.2–0.4 fair; 0.4–0.6 moderate; 0.6–0.8 substantial; >0.8 near-perfect.
